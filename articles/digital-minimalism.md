Digital minimalism isn't about throwing your smartphone into the ocean and moving to a cabin in the woods. It's a philosophy of technology use where you focus your online time on a small number of carefully selected activities that strongly support the things you value.

The endless scrolling, the constant notifications, the fear of missing out—these are symptoms of a digital life lived *by default*, not *by design*. 

By curating your digital environment, you transform your phone from a slot machine of distractions into a **precision tool**.

> "Keep the tools, remove the traps."

Start by auditing your apps. Does this app serve your long-term goals, or does it harvest your attention for profit?
