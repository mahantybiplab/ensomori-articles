We wear multitasking as a badge of honor, but the science is clear: the human brain cannot focus on two complex tasks at once. What we call multitasking is actually **"context switching,"** and it comes at a high cognitive cost.

Every time you switch from a deep task to a notification, it takes an average of *23 minutes* to return to your original state of flow. By checking Instagram for "just a second," you aren't just losing that second—you're losing your momentum.

True power lies in mono-tasking. Doing one thing, fully, until it is finished.

### Try This Today:
1. Put your phone in another room.
2. Pick one task.
3. Do it for 30 minutes without stopping.
